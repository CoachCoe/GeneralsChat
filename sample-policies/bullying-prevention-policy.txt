SCHOOL DISTRICT BULLYING PREVENTION AND INTERVENTION POLICY

Policy Number: DISC-001
Effective Date: September 1, 2024
Last Updated: November 1, 2024

SECTION 1: POLICY STATEMENT

Our school district is committed to providing a safe, respectful learning environment free from bullying, harassment, and intimidation. All students have the right to feel safe and supported at school. This policy establishes procedures for preventing, identifying, and responding to bullying incidents.

SECTION 2: DEFINITION OF BULLYING

Bullying is defined as repeated, unwanted aggressive behavior that involves a real or perceived power imbalance. Bullying includes, but is not limited to:

A. Physical bullying: Hitting, kicking, pushing, spitting, or damaging someone's property
B. Verbal bullying: Name-calling, teasing, taunting, threatening, or making inappropriate comments
C. Social/Relational bullying: Spreading rumors, excluding someone from a group, or damaging someone's reputation
D. Cyberbullying: Using electronic devices or social media to harass, threaten, embarrass, or target another person

SECTION 3: REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

All incidents of suspected bullying must be reported immediately to school administrators.

A. MANDATORY REPORTERS
All school staff members are mandatory reporters and must:
- Report any witnessed or suspected bullying to the principal or designee within 2 hours of observation
- Document the incident in writing within 24 hours
- Maintain confidentiality while ensuring student safety

B. STUDENT AND PARENT REPORTING
- Students are encouraged to report bullying to any trusted adult
- Parents/guardians may report concerns to teachers, counselors, or administrators
- Anonymous reports can be submitted through the district website or school office

C. TIMELINE FOR REPORTING
- Witnessed incidents: Report within 2 hours
- Student reports: Document same day
- Parent reports: Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours

SECTION 4: INVESTIGATION PROCEDURES

Upon receipt of a bullying report, the following steps must be taken:

A. IMMEDIATE RESPONSE (0-24 HOURS)
1. Ensure immediate safety of the victim
2. Separate involved parties if necessary
3. Notify the principal or designee
4. Begin preliminary fact-gathering
5. Contact parents/guardians of all involved students

B. INVESTIGATION TIMELINE (2-5 SCHOOL DAYS)
1. Interview the victim separately and privately
2. Interview the alleged perpetrator separately
3. Interview witnesses individually
4. Review any physical evidence (texts, emails, photos, videos)
5. Review school surveillance footage if applicable
6. Document all findings thoroughly

C. INVESTIGATION COMPLETION (Within 10 SCHOOL DAYS)
1. Complete written investigation report
2. Determine whether bullying occurred based on evidence
3. Develop action plan if bullying is substantiated
4. Schedule follow-up meetings with all parties

SECTION 5: NOTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

A. PARENT/GUARDIAN NOTIFICATION
Parents/guardians of both the victim and perpetrator must be notified:
- Initial notification: Within 24 hours of report
- Investigation findings: Within 2 school days of completion
- Action plan: Before implementation

B. DOCUMENTATION
All notifications must be documented including:
- Date and time of contact
- Method of contact (phone, email, in-person)
- Summary of information shared
- Parent/guardian response

SECTION 6: CONSEQUENCES AND INTERVENTIONS

Consequences for bullying behavior will be age-appropriate and consider:
- Severity and frequency of behavior
- Student's prior disciplinary history
- Impact on the victim
- Student's understanding of the behavior

A. FIRST OFFENSE (Low Severity)
- Parent conference
- Written apology
- Loss of privileges
- Counseling referral
- Behavior contract

B. SECOND OFFENSE OR MODERATE SEVERITY
- 1-3 day in-school suspension
- Mandatory counseling
- Restorative justice conference
- Monitoring plan
- Parent meetings

C. THIRD OFFENSE OR SEVERE INCIDENT
- 3-10 day out-of-school suspension
- Recommendation for alternative placement
- Possible expulsion hearing
- Law enforcement notification if applicable

SECTION 7: VICTIM SUPPORT

The school will provide support to victims of bullying including:
- Immediate safety plan
- Counseling services
- Academic accommodations if needed
- Regular check-ins with designated staff member
- Peer support programs
- Schedule modifications if necessary

SECTION 8: PREVENTION PROGRAMS

The district will implement comprehensive prevention strategies:
- Annual anti-bullying assemblies for all students
- Monthly classroom lessons on respect and inclusion
- Staff training on bullying identification and response
- Parent education workshops (2 per year minimum)
- Peer mediation and mentorship programs
- Positive Behavioral Interventions and Supports (PBIS)

SECTION 9: MONITORING AND FOLLOW-UP

A. SHORT-TERM FOLLOW-UP (1-2 weeks after incident)
- Check in with victim and perpetrator separately
- Monitor behavior and interactions
- Assess effectiveness of interventions
- Adjust support plan if needed

B. LONG-TERM MONITORING (30-60 days)
- Monthly check-ins with involved students
- Review academic and behavioral progress
- Communicate with parents/guardians
- Document ongoing status

SECTION 10: CYBERBULLYING SPECIFIC PROVISIONS

For incidents involving technology or social media:
- School has jurisdiction if behavior impacts school environment
- Students must provide access to relevant electronic communications
- Screenshots and digital evidence will be preserved
- Technology privileges may be restricted
- Law enforcement may be contacted for illegal activity

SECTION 11: RETALIATION PROHIBITED

Retaliation against anyone who reports bullying or participates in an investigation is strictly prohibited and will result in:
- Immediate disciplinary action
- Potential law enforcement involvement
- Enhanced monitoring and support for the victim

SECTION 12: CONFIDENTIALITY

To the extent possible while ensuring student safety:
- Investigations will be conducted confidentially
- Information shared on need-to-know basis only
- Student privacy rights will be protected
- Records maintained according to district policy

SECTION 13: APPEAL PROCESS

Parents/guardians may appeal disciplinary decisions by:
1. Submitting written appeal to superintendent within 5 school days
2. Attending appeal hearing within 10 school days
3. Receiving written decision within 5 school days of hearing

SECTION 14: ANNUAL REVIEW

- School board

- Parent advisory committee
- Student representatives
